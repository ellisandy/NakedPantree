## 4. Description

```
Naked Pantree is a pantry tracker for real homes — the kitchen cabinet, the garage fridge, the chest freezer in the barn. Know what you have, before you're back from the store with a third jar of mustard.

Add an item, pick a location, set an expiry date if it has one. The app tells you what's running low and what's about to go off, before it becomes a problem. Share a household with whoever else lives there and the inventory stays in sync — no accounts, no servers, just iCloud.

Built for households, not magazine kitchens.

Features
• Multiple locations — pantry, fridge, freezer, garage, barn, anywhere food lives.
• Expiring Soon — one list across every location, sorted by what goes off first.
• Recently Added — see what just got logged, in case you forgot what you bought.
• Search — find anything across every location, instantly.
• Photos — primary shot plus reference photos for ingredient lists or labels.
• Expiry reminders — a local notification a few days before food goes off.
• iCloud sharing — invite a partner or roommate, edits sync between devices.
• iPhone, iPad, and Apple Silicon Mac.

Privacy
Your inventory lives in your iCloud account. There are no third-party servers, no accounts to create, no analytics following you around. Sharing happens through an iCloud invite — only people you invite can see the list.

Why it exists
Most homes have food in three or four places. Most pantry apps assume one. Naked Pantree was built around the messy reality: an outdoor freezer that nobody opens, a garage fridge full of beverages, a basement shelf nobody remembers. One inventory, every spot.
```
